\section{Conclusion}

This sample paper asked a deployment question: when a battery-constrained quadruped can spend energy both to move and to adapt, when should it adapt? On the synthetic benchmark used for this repository demonstration, low-battery regimes favor a battery-gated forward-only adaptation rule over always-on adaptation, while high-battery regimes keep always-on adaptation competitive. That pattern supports a scheduling interpretation rather than a universal superiority claim.

More importantly for the repository itself, the sample leaves behind the full artifact path that the workflow is supposed to produce: early-stage research notes, an auto-review summary, a narrative handoff, a paper plan, LaTeX sources, and a compiled PDF. A real follow-on project can now swap the synthetic benchmark for a real locomotion stack without having to guess what the final artifact tree should look like, while preserving the same local file structure.
