\section{Synthetic Benchmark}

The repository does not include a locomotion codebase, a simulator, or battery instrumentation. To complete the end-to-end workflow honestly, we therefore instantiate a synthetic benchmark whose only purpose is to make the scheduling hypothesis concrete and to leave a realistic artifact tree. All values used in this section come from \texttt{results/demo\_benchmark.json}.

The benchmark spans two shift families, terrain shift and payload shift, under two battery regimes: a low-battery state at $25\%$ and a high-battery state at $80\%$. Each regime evaluates the four policies described above. For every policy we record mission return, distance traveled, cost of transport (CoT), falls per $100$m, number of adaptation invocations, and adaptation-compute energy in watt-hours.

The benchmark is designed to test one qualitative crossover. Low-battery regimes should punish always-on adaptation when its compute burden becomes non-trivial. High-battery regimes may still reward aggressive adaptation, because the controller can afford to spend more battery to maintain robustness. We further include an adaptation-cost sweep and a low-battery ablation to show how the crossover depends on the explicit cost model.
