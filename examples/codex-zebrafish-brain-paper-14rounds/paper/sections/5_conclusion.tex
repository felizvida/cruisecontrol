\section{Conclusion}

Taken together, these results support a clear statement about the adult zebrafish brain. Telencephalon and optic tectum occupy strongly distinct proteoform regimes, and the proteoforms that are most legible in the public top-down data are arranged in ways that match the known biology of those regions. That conclusion survives prevalence-adjusted overlap tests, duplicate-informed Chao2 and jackknife sensitivity analyses, MNAR-style low-intensity fills, family-size-preserving randomization, protein collapse, and an independent literature-derived composition panel. The PTM follow-up points in a more cautious direction: once public detectability proxies are taken seriously, the acetylation asymmetry is better read as hypothesis-generating than as a robust regional program. The evidence base remains public and limited. Even so, it is strong enough to turn a suggestive pilot result into an explicit and reusable claim about regional molecular organization in the adult zebrafish brain.
