# Archival Source Hunt

## Purpose

This memo records the direct-source hunt that followed the round 31 historiographical pass. The aim was to move beyond general literature and identify the exact archival, printed, and newspaper materials most likely to deepen the Frederick argument.

The hunt focused on three targets:

1. the 1811-1812 court-martial record
2. Frederick newspapers
3. Frederick legal papers, especially around Roger Brooke Taney and John Hanson Thomas

## Best Direct Leads Found

### 1. Printed Court-Martial Report

- [Report of the court-martial ... for the trial of Brigadier General James Wilkinson, 1811](https://www.themorgan.org/printed-books/121424)

Why it matters:

- This is the cleanest direct lead on the printed Frederick proceeding itself.
- The Morgan record dates it from the Inspector's Office in Washington on 19 February 1812, just after Madison approved the acquittal.
- The pamphlet is short, only thirty-seven pages, which makes it realistic as the next source to retrieve in full.

Best use:

- strengthen the Frederick section with the court's own language
- clarify the charges, procedural structure, and wording of acquittal
- reduce reliance on later retellings

### 2. Court-Martial Witness Summons Material

- [Walter Jones, Jr. summons Thomas Power to testify in Wilkinson's trial, 21 June 1811](https://mohistory.mobiusconsortium.org/repositories/2/archival_objects/10785)

Why it matters:

- This is a direct procedural record tied to Wilkinson's trial.
- It shows witness gathering before the Frederick phase was fully visible in the Madison and Adams correspondence used in the current paper.

Best use:

- expand the evidentiary chain around witness procurement
- show that the Frederick proceeding sat inside a wider documentary net, not a narrow local bubble

### 3. Frederick Newspaper Holdings and Abstracts

- [Maryland State Archives guide to Bartgis's Republican Gazette](https://speccol.msa.maryland.gov/pages/newspaper/number.aspx?id=275)
- [Maryland State Archives collection page for Bartgis's Republican Gazette](https://speccol.msa.maryland.gov/pages/speccol/collection.aspx?speccol=3687)
- [Maryland State Archives collection page for the Frederick-Town Herald](https://speccol.msa.maryland.gov/pages/speccol/collection.aspx?speccol=2829)
- [Maryland State Archives newspaper abstracts landing page for Frederick County, 1811-1815](https://msa.maryland.gov/megafile/msa/speccol/sc3500/sc3520/000700/000743/html/frederick1811.html)
- [FamilySearch catalog entry for F. Edward Wright, *Newspaper Abstracts of Frederick County, 1811-1815*](https://www.familysearch.org/search/catalog/588907)
- [Google Books record for Wright's abstract volume](https://books.google.com/books/about/Newspaper_Abstracts_of_Frederick_County.html?id=1NjtzwEACAAJ)

Why they matter:

- The archives confirm that Frederick's newspapers survive on film and in abstracted form.
- The Maryland State Archives explicitly points to Wright's abstract volume for 1811-1815, which is the shortest path to local press references without first handling every surviving issue.

Limits:

- I could not directly retrieve OCR or scans of the 1811 issues because the Library of Congress issue pages are currently behind a Cloudflare challenge in this environment.
- That means the newspaper line is now a strong holdings-and-abstracts lead, but not yet a mined set of quoted Frederick articles.

Best use:

- mine Wright's abstracts for Wilkinson, Taney, Thomas, court-martial, and Frederick-town references
- then pull the corresponding issues from film or repository copies if the abstracts turn up high-value notices

### 3a. What the newspaper trail already yields

Even before retrieving Wright's volume itself, the newspaper route has started to pay off in one concrete place.

- The Maryland State Archives source page for John Hanson Thomas identifies two \textit{Frederick-Town Herald} notices: 6 May 1815 and 27 May 1815.
- The companion biography page preserves one line from the 6 May 1815 notice, saying that Thomas ``has left a chasm in society which can not be filled.''

Why it matters:

- It confirms that Frederick's own press treated one of Wilkinson's counsel as a figure of public consequence.
- That gives the Thomas/Taney paragraph a firmer local basis than a later county-memory tradition alone.
- It also suggests that Wright's abstract volume is likely to be productive for the immediate afterlife of the Wilkinson defense, even if the surviving notices we can reach now are about Thomas rather than Wilkinson directly.

Limit:

- This remains one step removed from the original issue. The Maryland State Archives pages index and quote the notice, but do not reproduce the full newspaper image in this environment.

Best use:

- strengthen the Frederick legal-culture paragraph with a locally grounded indication of Thomas's standing
- prioritize the 6 May and 27 May 1815 \textit{Frederick-Town Herald} issues if fuller newspaper retrieval becomes possible

### 4. Taney and Frederick Legal Papers

- [Federal Judicial Center: Roger Brooke Taney](https://www.fjc.gov/history/judges/taney-roger-brooke)
- [UVA Law Library finding aid for Roger B. Taney legal papers](https://archives.law.virginia.edu/resources/inventory-legal-papers-roger-b-taney)
- [Heritage Frederick Research Center](https://frederickhistory.org/research-center/)
- [Maryland Center for History and Culture Frederick County Historical Society collection](https://mdhistory.libraryhost.com/repositories/2/resources/492)

Why they matter:

- The FJC page is the clearest high-level guide to where the Taney papers are.
- The UVA Law finding aid is especially important because it identifies miscellaneous legal documents from Taney's Frederick law practice from 1805 to 1812, exactly the window needed for the Wilkinson defense story.
- Heritage Frederick and MCHC remain the strongest local institutional leads for Frederick-family and county-level legal material.

Best use:

- verify whether the Wilkinson defense left traces in Frederick legal correspondence
- strengthen the Taney paragraph with manuscript evidence rather than memory alone

### 5. Frederick Legal Context Around John Hanson Thomas and Taney

- [Maryland State Archives biography of John Hanson Thomas](https://msa.maryland.gov/megafile/msa/speccol/sc3500/sc3520/013900/013936/html/13936bio.html)
- [Maryland State Archives mirrored Catholic Encyclopedia entry on Roger Brooke Taney](https://msa.maryland.gov/megafile/msa/speccol/sc3500/sc3520/001500/001500/html/catholic.html)
- [Wikisource page of the Catholic Encyclopedia passage](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Page%3ACatholic_Encyclopedia%2C_volume_14.djvu/499)

Why they matter:

- The John Hanson Thomas biography is more useful than it first appears. It states directly that Thomas and Taney represented Wilkinson in Frederick, won acquittal, and refused payment because they believed him guilty. It also opens a further local-political path by tying Thomas and Taney to later newspaper conflict inside Frederick.
- The Taney encyclopedia passage preserves the same no-fee defense tradition in a widely circulated older account.

Best use:

- strengthen the legal-support portion of the Frederick story
- connect the Wilkinson defense to Frederick's later partisan and legal culture

## What We Can Use Right Now

The strongest immediately usable additions are:

- the Morgan court-martial pamphlet record
- the Walter Jones witness-summons record
- the War Department receipt placing Wilkinson and his suite in Frederick Town on 2 December 1800
- Jefferson's 20 January 1808 special message on the Wilkinson inquiry
- the FJC and UVA Law Taney-paper leads
- the John Hanson Thomas biography
- the Library of Congress directory entry for the \textit{Frederick-Town Herald}
- the Maryland State Archives newspaper-holdings and abstract references

These do not yet replace a full archive trip or a full pamphlet retrieval, but they move the project from vague future-work language to a sharply defined source map.

Two items from the supplied source packet are especially worth carrying into the paper itself.

- The War Department receipt demonstrates that Frederick Town was already part of Wilkinson's military-administrative geography in 1800, before the famous 1804 and 1811 documents.
- Jefferson's 20 January 1808 special message shows that executive support for Wilkinson was paired with formal inquiry, Daniel Clark's evidence, and an explicit promise of ``rigorous impartiality.''
