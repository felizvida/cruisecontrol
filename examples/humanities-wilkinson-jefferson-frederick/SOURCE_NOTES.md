# Source Notes

## Core Primary Sources

- [Jefferson to Wilkinson / Bates forwarding context, 23 February 1801](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/01-33-02-0083)
  Early operational trust linking Jefferson, Wilkinson, and Meriwether Lewis.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 18 January 1802](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/01-36-02-0245)
  Scientific and patronage exchange.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 11 June 1804](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/01-43-02-0468-0001)
  Dinner invitation evidence showing social access.

- [Wilkinson to Adams from Frederick Town, 12 September 1804](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Adams/99-02-02-5052)
  Early evidence for Frederick as a place of reputational management.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 22 October 1805](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/01-47-02-0475)
  Buffalo-hide map, frontier intelligence, and attachment language.

- [Jefferson to Wilkinson, 3 February 1807](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/99-01-02-5012)
  The key support letter during the Burr crisis.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 20 March 1807](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/99-01-02-5318)
  Wilkinson's emotional reply to Jefferson's backing.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 15 April 1807](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/99-01-02-5458)
  Appeal for orders and direction.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 21 January 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/03-03-02-0246)
  Memoir as political self-defense.

- [Jefferson to Wilkinson, 10 March 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/03-03-02-0329)
  Jefferson's strongest private reassurance.

- [Wilkinson to Madison from Frederick Town, 9 October 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Madison/03-03-02-0571)
  Frederick as the trial venue of survival.

- [Madison to Jefferson, 7 February 1812](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Madison/03-04-02-0182)
  Formal approval of the Frederick-town acquittal.

- [Jefferson to Wilkinson, 25 June 1818](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/03-13-02-0105)
  Jefferson's estimate of Wilkinson's services remains unaltered.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 4 August 1818](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/03-13-02-0200)
  Retrospective gratitude and attachment.

## Interpretive Limits

- The paper does not claim a newly discovered manuscript.
- The local-history argument is a synthesis from dispersed records.
- The coding outputs are reproducible aids, not substitutes for archival reading.
