# Source Notes

## Core Primary Sources

- [War Department receipt for forage and subsistence for Wilkinson and his suite, Frederick Town, 2 December 1800](https://wardepartmentpapers.org/s/home/item/79117)
  Earliest direct administrative trace placing Wilkinson in Frederick Town itself.

- [Jefferson to Wilkinson / Bates forwarding context, 23 February 1801](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/01-33-02-0083)
  Early operational trust linking Jefferson, Wilkinson, and Meriwether Lewis.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 18 January 1802](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/01-36-02-0245)
  Scientific and patronage exchange.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 11 June 1804](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/01-43-02-0468-0001)
  Dinner invitation evidence showing social access.

- [Wilkinson to Adams from Frederick Town, 12 September 1804](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Adams/99-02-02-5052)
  Early evidence for Frederick as a place of reputational management.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 22 October 1805](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/01-47-02-0475)
  Buffalo-hide map, frontier intelligence, and attachment language.

- [Jefferson to Wilkinson, 3 February 1807](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/99-01-02-5012)
  The key support letter during the Burr crisis.

- [Jefferson, Special Message to the House of Representatives on the Wilkinson inquiry, 20 January 1808](https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/special-message-2197)
  Shows Jefferson routing the allegations into a court of inquiry, acknowledging Daniel Clark's evidence, and promising rigorous impartiality.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 20 March 1807](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/99-01-02-5318)
  Wilkinson's emotional reply to Jefferson's backing.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 15 April 1807](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/99-01-02-5458)
  Appeal for orders and direction.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 21 January 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/03-03-02-0246)
  Memoir as political self-defense.

- [Jefferson to Wilkinson, 10 March 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/03-03-02-0329)
  Jefferson's strongest private reassurance.

- [Eustis to Madison, 13 August 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Madison/03-03-02-0487)
  Early War Department evidence that Frederick Town was the intended court-martial venue.

- [Madison to Eustis, 15 September 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Madison/03-03-02-0550)
  Presidential acknowledgment that the Frederick tribunal already possessed Wilkinson's case.

- [Eustis to Madison, 25 September 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Madison/03-03-02-0558)
  Witness-management evidence showing the War Department supporting the Frederick proceedings.

- [Jabeil Kinson to Adams from Frederick Town, 2 October 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Adams/99-02-02-5689)
  Interrogatories sent from Frederick Town by order of the general court-martial.

- [Wilkinson to Madison from Frederick Town, 9 October 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Madison/03-03-02-0571)
  Frederick as the trial venue of survival.

- [Adams to Wilkinson, 12 October 1811](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Adams/99-02-02-5694)
  High-status testimonial support flowing back into the Frederick defense record.

- [Madison to Jefferson, 7 February 1812](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Madison/03-04-02-0182)
  Formal approval of the Frederick-town acquittal.

- [Jefferson to Wilkinson, 25 June 1818](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/03-13-02-0105)
  Jefferson's estimate of Wilkinson's services remains unaltered.

- [Wilkinson to Jefferson, 4 August 1818](https://founders.archives.gov/documents/Jefferson/03-13-02-0200)
  Retrospective gratitude and attachment.

## Direct Archival and Newspaper Leads

- [Report of the court-martial ... for the trial of Brigadier General James Wilkinson, 1811](https://www.themorgan.org/printed-books/121424)
  Printed court-martial report dated from the Inspector's Office, Washington, 19 February 1812. The strongest direct lead on the Frederick proceeding itself.

- [Walter Jones, Jr. summons Thomas Power to testify in Wilkinson's trial, 21 June 1811](https://mohistory.mobiusconsortium.org/repositories/2/archival_objects/10785)
  A witness-summons document tied to the Wilkinson trial, useful for extending the procedural chain before the Frederick correspondence now in the corpus.

- [Federal Judicial Center: Roger Brooke Taney](https://www.fjc.gov/history/judges/taney-roger-brooke)
  Best portal to the major Taney manuscript collections, including Frederick holdings.

- [UVA Law Library finding aid for Roger B. Taney legal papers](https://archives.law.virginia.edu/resources/inventory-legal-papers-roger-b-taney)
  Identifies Frederick-practice legal documents from 1805-1812, the key window for Wilkinson's trial.

- [Maryland State Archives guide to Bartgis's Republican Gazette](https://speccol.msa.maryland.gov/pages/newspaper/number.aspx?id=275)
  Confirms Frederick newspaper holdings and points to Wright's 1811-1815 abstract volume.

- [Maryland State Archives guide to the Frederick-Town Herald](https://speccol.msa.maryland.gov/pages/speccol/collection.aspx?speccol=2829)
  Confirms holdings and abstract coverage for the second main Frederick paper.

- [Maryland State Archives biography of John Hanson Thomas](https://msa.maryland.gov/megafile/msa/speccol/sc3500/sc3520/013900/013936/html/13936bio.html)
  Useful for the Wilkinson defense team in Frederick and the later local political context around Thomas and Taney. Also preserves a Frederick-Town Herald tribute of 6 May 1815 calling Thomas's death a loss that ``has left a chasm in society which can not be filled.''

- [Maryland State Archives biography of Thomas Johnson](https://msa.maryland.gov/megafile/msa/speccol/sc3500/sc3520/000700/000743/html/743bio.html)
  Useful as background for Frederick's Jeffersonian political world, though not a core Wilkinson document.

- [Maryland State Archives sources page for John Hanson Thomas](https://msa.maryland.gov/megafile/msa/speccol/sc3500/sc3520/013900/013936/html/13936sources.html)
  Confirms two Frederick-Town Herald notices for Thomas, 6 May 1815 and 27 May 1815, and points directly to Wright's abstract volume.

## Polemical and Archival Guides

- [Wilkinson, James. *Memoirs of My Own Times*](https://archive.org/details/memoirsofmyownti01wilk)
  Important as Wilkinson's own self-defensive printed voice and part of the 1811 memoir campaign's broader public afterlife.

- [Clark, Daniel. *Proofs of the Corruption of Gen. James Wilkinson*](https://archive.org/details/proofsofcorrupti00clar)
  The strongest contemporary anti-Wilkinson printed case in the source packet; useful for understanding the scale of the accusations the executive had to manage.

- [National Archives, Record Group 153 court-martial case files](https://www.archives.gov/research/military/army/court-martial-case-files)
  The clearest federal route to the surviving Wilkinson court-martial file.

- [Library of Congress, Thomas Jefferson Papers collection guide](https://www.loc.gov/collections/thomas-jefferson-papers/about-this-collection/)
  Best high-level guide to the Jefferson manuscript collection.

- [Library of Congress correspondence list for Thomas Jefferson and James Wilkinson](https://www.loc.gov/item/mtjbib009416/)
  Useful as a manuscript finding aid even when individual item pages are harder to access.

- [Library of Congress correspondence list, 6 March 1811](https://www.loc.gov/item/mtjbib020535/)
  Another Jefferson-Wilkinson finding aid useful for locating manuscript traffic around the rehabilitation phase.

## Interpretive Limits

- The paper does not claim a newly discovered manuscript.
- The local-history argument is a synthesis from dispersed records.
- The coding outputs are reproducible aids, not substitutes for archival reading.
