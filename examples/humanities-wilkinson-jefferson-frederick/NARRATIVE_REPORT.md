# Narrative Report

## Working Title

**Useful to Jefferson, Vindicated at Frederick-Town: James Wilkinson and the Geography of Political Survival**

## Research Goal

Produce a humanities-paper example that feels like a serious local-history commission rather than a generic biography recap.

The paper needs to answer three questions at once:

1. What kind of personal relationship did Wilkinson actually have with Jefferson?
2. How did Wilkinson survive politically despite scandal?
3. Why should Frederick, Maryland care?

## Chosen Thesis

The evidence does not support a simple friendship story.

The stronger claim is:

- Jefferson and Wilkinson were bound by western utility, crisis management, and later mutual esteem
- Wilkinson survived because Jefferson's support, Madison's confirmation, and Frederick's legal and military setting turned scandal into procedure
- Frederick was not just where the story happened to stop; it was one of the places that made survival possible

## Why This Makes A Good Humanities Example

- It uses primary correspondence rather than only biography.
- It includes a real local-history angle.
- It uses computation in a disciplined way: source coding, network edges, and timeline generation.
- It produces a paper package that a local historical society could actually review.

## Main Discovery

The most interesting finding is not that Frederick appears in the story at all.

It is that Frederick appears in multiple distinct functions:

1. as part of Wilkinson's older Maryland geography near Monocacy
2. as a military-logistical site linked to Jefferson's western projects through the barracks
3. as the trial geography where Wilkinson's career was repaired enough to continue

That turns Frederick from background scenery into a historical hinge.
