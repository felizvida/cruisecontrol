# Discovery Memo

## Main Finding

Frederick is not incidental scenery in the Wilkinson story.

It is one of the places where his career became survivable.

## Why

The coded corpus shows:

- `17` total records
- `14` primary records and `3` local-context records
- `6` Frederick-linked records, or about `35%` of the corpus
- Frederick is the third-heaviest node in the relationship network, behind only Wilkinson and Jefferson

## New Local Synthesis

The paper's strongest local contribution is the claim that Frederick worked in three different registers:

1. **Maryland footing**
   Wilkinson already had a Monocacy connection before the crisis years.

2. **Jeffersonian infrastructure**
   The Frederick Barracks touched the same western-project world that linked Jefferson, Lewis, and Wilkinson.

3. **Procedural survival**
   Frederick-town was where Wilkinson's scandal became a trial record, a local legal defense, and then an approved acquittal.

## Bottom Line

If the local historical society wants one sentence for the exhibit wall, it is this:

**Wilkinson did not simply pass through Frederick. Frederick helped make his political survival possible.**
