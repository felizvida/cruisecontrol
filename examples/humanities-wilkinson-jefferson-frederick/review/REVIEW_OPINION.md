# Review Opinion

## Overall Verdict

**Score**: `8.4 / 10`
**Recommendation**: `Accept` for a local-history journal, public-history review, or short scholarly note on the early republic

## Summary

This paper works because it does not try to launder Wilkinson into a hero. Instead, it asks a sharper question: how did such a compromised figure remain politically survivable, and what role did Frederick play in that process? The answer is convincing. The paper shows that Jefferson's relationship with Wilkinson was structured more by utility and protection than by intimacy, and it gives Frederick a real analytical role rather than a ceremonial local mention.

The computational layer is well judged. The counts and figures are modest, transparent, and appropriate for humanities evidence. They strengthen the paper without pretending to settle it.

## Main Strengths

1. The paper asks a better question than a simple moral biography.
2. Frederick is treated as an analytical hinge, not a local ornament.
3. Jefferson's support of Wilkinson is shown through concrete letters rather than insinuation.
4. The complete package is unusually reproducible for a humanities example.

## Main Weaknesses

1. The local-source layer includes public-history and later historical writing, not just manuscript archives.
2. The computational counts are useful but necessarily heuristic.
3. The argument is a synthesis, not a claim of brand-new archival discovery.

## Recommendation

This paper should be pitched as:

- a Frederick-centered early-republic case study
- a public-history note with scholarly apparatus
- a reproducible humanities-methods example

It should not be pitched as:

- a final verdict on Wilkinson's guilt or innocence in every scandal
- a purely quantitative digital-history paper
- a newly discovered archival breakthrough
