## Summary

After twenty-eight documented rounds across the reconstructed and live continuation loops, this paper reads like a venue-ready humanities case study rather than a good repository demo that happens to have a paper attached. The most important improvement is not cosmetic. The argument is now more trustworthy because it makes the evidence ladder explicit, defines what it means by a ``personal relationship,'' shows how Jeffersonian reassurance became Madisonian ratification, and tests the Frederick claim through several compact robustness checks.

The paper's central achievement is interpretive precision. It does not try to rescue Wilkinson morally, and it does not pretend to have uncovered a lost manuscript that changes everything. Instead, it makes a narrower and stronger claim: Jefferson's relationship with Wilkinson was politically personal, Madison's action mattered to its institutional afterlife, and Frederick was the dominant local place in the documentary chain through which that survivability became procedural. The computations remain modest, but they are now better integrated into the evidentiary structure of the paper.
